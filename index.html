About Me

Hi! I'm a prospective Ph.D. student in Neuroscience. I am fascinated by different forms of intelligence, and hope to combine experimental and theoretical approaches to push our understanding of learning. I am currently working with Dr. Sam Gershman to study principles of learning and memory in single-celled organisms.

When not doing research, I explore my creative side through ballet, embroidery, and philosophy.

Work

Learning and memory in single-celled organisms

Our central question explores what types of information are encoded during learning and where this information is stored. We study simple forms of learning, such as habituation and conditioning, in unicellular organisms. By varying parameters like timescales, intensity, and frequency, we investigate these fundamental questions.

We develop mathematical models to understand the basic principles of learning, while using molecular data to inform our theoretical framework. This bidirectional approach allows us to understand how computational models translate to molecular processes, while experimental data continuously shapes our theoretical understanding.
